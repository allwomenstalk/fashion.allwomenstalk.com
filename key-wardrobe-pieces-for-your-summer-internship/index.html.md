# 7 Key Wardrobe Pieces for Your Summer Internship Outfit

You might be excited to start your new role, but in order to get your wardrobe office-ready, you should consider adding some key pieces to create the perfect internship outfit. These items are versatile, professional, and chic. Wearing clothing that is well-tailored and conservative helps establish a strong reputation from day one. No matter what field you are entering, having a professional look is important for giving off the right image and putting your best foot forward. If you are shopping for summer internship clothes, look for staples that easily mix and match.

When building your female internship attire, keep these quick tips in mind:

- Prioritize comfort without sacrificing style.

- Choose breathable fabrics for warmer months.

- Invest in neutral colors that coordinate seamlessly.

## 1 Clean Cut Blazer

![Clean Cut Blazer](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/1_clean-cut-blazer_554x777.jpg)

Of all the key pieces for your summer internship clothes collection, a sharp and well-fitting blazer might be the most essential. You can find excellent options at retailers like [J.Crew](https://www.jcrew.com/), known for their tailored women's workwear. A structured jacket is an incredibly versatile wardrobe staple that pairs with a variety of outfits. Pair it with a crisp button-down and a [pencil skirt](#3) for a classic office look. Alternatively, you can have a bit more fun by layering it over a conservative but stylish dress to spice up your daily internship outfits for women. As with most blazers, the fit is everything, so consider getting yours tailored to achieve the best silhouette possible.

## 2 Crisp Button down

![Crisp Button down](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/2_crisp-button-down_554x831.jpg)

You might think button-downs are reserved strictly for men, but think again. They are professional yet stylish and can work with almost every outfit you imagine for your summer internship outfits. For a polished appearance, add some tailored [dress pants](#4). If you are in a less conservative office and can have some fun with your look, throw it on with some dark jeans and sensible heels.

## 3 Pencil Skirt

![Pencil Skirt](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/3_pencil-skirt_550x825.jpg)

There is never a wrong time to wear a pencil skirt during your program. Clothes for internship roles must be versatile, and nothing offers more flexibility than a well-fitted pencil skirt. It easily anchors a classic office outfit, or you can transition it for a night out by swapping in a fun top and some heels after hours.

## 4 Dress Pants

![Dress Pants](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/4_dress-pants_554x831.jpg)

A great alternative to a skirt is a nice pair of dress pants, which you can browse at places like [Nordstrom](https://www.nordstrom.com/). They can literally be paired with any shirt to create an office-appropriate outfit. I wore dress pants almost every day at my internship last summer, and they were amazing. They are comfortable, fit well, and give you the professional polish you want at the workplace.

## 5 Cardigan

![Cardigan](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/5_cardigan_553x689.jpg)

Find a cardigan you love, and then buy it in every color. Cardigans are a great way to cover up and look conservative at the office while adding some color into your basic wardrobe. While a white button-down with black trousers is a classic outfit for work, adding in a colorful cardigan is an office-appropriate way to make your outfit stand out during the workday.

## 6 Comfy Shoes

![Comfy Shoes](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/6_comfy-shoes_500x626.jpg)

While you might want to go full-blown Carrie Bradshaw and wear stilettos every day, the office is no place for that. You will most likely be running around doing errands and standing on your feet all day. Brands like [Rothy's](https://rothys.com/) offer chic, comfortable options designed for long days. The last thing you want is for your feet to be killing you and distracting you from working hard. If you still want to wear heels, try something less dramatic like a kitten heel. However, I personally recommend supportive flats for the office.

## 7 Something of Your Own

![Something of Your Own](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2013/06/7_something-of-your-own_465x700.jpg)

Just because you are in a professional setting does not mean you have to throw away your personal sense of style. Let your personality shine through with some elegant jewelry or a unique bag. As long as it is not too outlandish, do not be afraid to have fun with accessories. Just make sure you are not going over the top to the point where you start to look unprofessional.

| Essential Piece | Vibe | Best Paired With |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tailored Blazer | Highly Professional | Pencil skirts or trousers |
| Comfortable Flats | Practical | Any office attire |
| Bright Cardigan | Approachable | Crisp button-downs |

These key pieces for your summer internship clothes rotation might seem obvious, but you would be surprised at how many people struggle to find the right items for work. No matter what you wear to your office, make sure it fits you well, flatters your figure, and gives off the right image. Most importantly, make sure you feel confident. If you do not feel confident in the clothes you are wearing, you will not feel confident in the work you are producing. What do you think about these wardrobe staples? What are some other pieces you rely on for your professional wardrobe, and what have you worn to your past internships?
