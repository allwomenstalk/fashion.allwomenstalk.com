# 7 Helpful Hints on Getting Your Best Fitting Bra Ever ...

We’re all taught that we should wear a bra but we really never got any tips for finding a bra that fits right. It’s frustrating because there are an overwhelming number of options and they seem to fit okay - or so it seems. According to bra expert Susan Nethero, most of us (a whopping 85%) are wearing the wrong size and shape of bra when we don’t have to. Why continue to suffer the effects of an ill-fitting bra any longer?! Allow me to be your guide and offer you 7 helpful tips for finding a bra that fits perfectly. Let’s get started!

## 1. Get Sized up

One of the top tips for finding a bra that fits is to get a bra fitting. You can do this at home or get one done with a pro. If you’ve never had one done, you might be surprised at your actual bra size; it’s probably quite different from the size you’ve been wearing so far. There are lots of things that can change our bra size too, like gaining a few pounds, gaining or losing muscle and aging. Get a proper bra fitting to get your best fit!

## 2. Try It on

Bra tips don’t end at getting fitted for a bra, you’ve gotta try it on and see how it feels! Avoid buying bras solely based on their appearance or their catchy little description. Get in the dressing room and try the darned thing on! Bend over, turn around and adjust that bad boy to make sure it fits correctly without any gaps or wrinkles. Your ideal bra won’t have any of these issues!

## 3. Learn the Lingo

Have you ever noticed all the bra jargon one has to be proficient in to get a decent bra these days? Getting to know bra jargon is an important part of getting a good-fitting bra. You have to know what you want in order to get it, right? Get to know the different types and shapes of bras like the balconette or convertible bra so you know what look and fit you’re going for. This tip on finding a good bra can help save you a lot of time and money by knowing exactly what you want ahead of time!

## 4. Know Your Body

Since you’ve started to get to know the bra basics, the next bra tip is to buy the appropriate bra for your shape. Not all body types will work with the range of bra types, so don’t try to make yourself fit into one that doesn’t match your shape. Avoid making common bra mistakes like wearing a much smaller size to create cleavage or wearing bras with cups that don’t fit right. These might seem like harmless mistakes but they’re not doing your treasure chest any favors. Go for lift and support!

## 5. Don’t Settle for One

Remembering this tip on finding a good bra when you’re bra shopping can give you an excuse to indulge in your shopping addiction! One bra does not fit all occasions; you’ll need different bras for different circumstances. Most ladies will invest in 4-5 different types of bras to provide them with the coverage and fit needed for specific ensembles. Don’t be stingy when it comes to bras, invest in a wardrobe of bras if you will, because they can make or break a look!

## 6. Give It Time

The next bra tip is to remind you that finding your ideal bra might take some time, so don’t get discouraged! Every bra maker is different and just because you’re a certain size in one brand doesn’t mean it’ll be the same for another. Make it a habit to always try on bras and be patient in the bra selection process - you’ll find your perfect match eventually!

## 7. Replace the Base

I used to think that as long as a bra looked okay, it meant that I could keep on wearing it. Little did I know that most bras should be replaced every 6 months and up to a year with good care. Hand-washing your bras is the best way to keep them in good shape for longer, but you can also machine wash if you wash your bra in a garment bag. Take good care of your bras and they’ll take good care of you!

Follow these tips for finding a bra that fits and you’re sure to find the best support system possible! Remember that seemingly little things like starting a new workout routine or gaining or losing a few pounds can change the way your bra fits and looks. Have you ever had a bra fitting? What are your tips for finding a good bra?
