# 8 Classic Winter Fashions That Will Never Fail You ...

**Winter Fashions** change every year. However, there is a range of **winter fashions** and accessory items that will stand the test of time. These are the pieces in your wardrobe that should be labelled as ‘staples’ or ‘investment pieces’. When putting together a winter wardrobe, I like to start with the basics, and then build the rest of my wardrobe up with trend-based or seasonal accessories. Take a look at a few staple **winter fashions** below.

## 1. Camel Coat

A knee length wool coat in a camel colourway is the epitome of winter chic, and one of the most classic winter fashions you can own. While ‘camel’ has been a buzz colour in recent years, it has been a popular choice for decades. A camel coloured coat is also quite versatile, as the neutral colour allows it to be worn with a range of different outfits.

## 2. Long-Sleeved Breton Top

Popularised by the ever-stylish Coco Chanel, the Breton top is a wardrobe must-have. This striped style of top is timeless. In the winter months, choose a long-sleeved style made from heavier fabric. For a classically [feminine look](https://fashion.allwomenstalk.com/super-simple-tips-to-look-more-feminine/), team a navy and white Breton top with black skinny jeans, ballet pumps, and a navy pea coat.

## 3. Knee-High Boots

When it comes to winter fashions, you’ve got to consider your footwear. Boots are a must in the winter, and knee-high styles are not only timeless but flattering to most leg shapes as well. Knee-high boots look great with tucked in jeans or opaque tights, making them an ideal choice of winter footwear. Black and tan pairs will go with most outfits.

## 4. Leather Jacket

The leather jacket is another worthy investment piece. There are a range of styles to choose from, but the biker style is my personal favourite. Each season designers will reinterpret the leather jacket, but sticking to a fairly basic cut will ensure that yours lasts year in and year out.

## 5. Knit Cardigan

Cardigans are great for transitional dressing. They’re also ideal for layering under heavy wool coats in the winter. Boyfriend-style knit cardigans are ideal for creating a casual off-duty look, whereas fitted ones are much more refined. Both of these styles of winter fashions, however, can be counted as wardrobe staples.

## 6. Leopard Print Scarf

Like it or not, leopard is a timeless print. A leopard print scarf is also a great way to add extra detail to an outfit. Channel the screen sirens of the past and team yours with a twin-set and pencil skirt, or go for a rock-chic vibe and wear it with skinny jeans and a leather jacket. These types of accessories are not online timeless but versatile.

## 7. Felt Fedora

Adding a felt fedora to your winter look will not only add extra style, but added warmth as well. We lose a lot of heat through our heads, and wearing a hat in winter will help keep the warmth in. The fedora is a classic style, and one in a neutral black colour will easily work with a range of different winter fashions.

## 8. Navy Pea Coat

The navy pea coat with gold or brass buttons is a classic winter fashion item. It’s perfect for wearing during the day. Choose one that hits mid-thigh and team it with anything and everything, from shift dresses to skinny jeans.

These are just some examples of **winter fashions** that will never fail you. They’ve stood the test of time so far, and are sure to be a part of many winter wardrobes in the future. Remember, that's the key to true **winter fashions**: they never go out of style. They always look chic and glamorous. Are there any more timeless **winter fashions** that you’d like to add to this list?
