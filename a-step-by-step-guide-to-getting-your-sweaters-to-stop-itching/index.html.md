# A Step by Step Guide to Getting Your Sweaters to Stop Itching ...

It's the season of sweaters. Unfortunately, it's hard to wear them around when they make your skin itch. Of course, there's a cure for that! According to [Glamour](https://www.glamour.com/story/the-fixer-what-to-do-about-an?mbid=twitter_fashion), you should follow these steps to make your sweaters stop itching:

## 1. Soak It in Water

![Soak It in Water](https://img.allw.mn/content/g4/z9/s6zqv7hg5670300b001e2697933483_500x281.gif)

Grab your sweater and flip it inside out. After you do that, you should soak it in a mixture of cold water and white vinegar. Leave it there for 15 minutes, and then drain it.

## 2. Add Conditioner

![Add Conditioner](https://img.allw.mn/content/a7/qi/ojc5anrn56703037151dc492361164_500x250.gif)

While your sweater is still wet from the first step, you should grab some hair conditioner and rub it into the fibers of your sweater. Just don't use a brand that's too cheap, because it might contain wax that could ruin your outfit.

## 3. Let It Set

![Let It Set](https://img.allw.mn/content/cg/nk/ecjneqrv567030377733d992593186_500x281.gif)

Leave the conditioner on your sweater for around 30 minutes. Once that time is up, you should rinse it off with cold water.

## 4. Let It Dry

![Let It Dry](https://img.allw.mn/content/po/np/zqe6924n5670300c4f22c892736490_450x337.gif)

Then, you should place your sweater on a towel to let it air dry. You don't want to use a blowdryer for this.

## 5. Place in the Freezer

![Place in the Freezer](https://img.allw.mn/content/yi/lu/i758i19v5670300bd10cb117118427_480x271.gif)

Now it's time to fold your sweater up, place it in a large ziplock bag, and place it in the freezer. Leave it there overnight. "The vinegar and conditioner softens the fibers, while the freezer keeps the shorter fibers from sticking out, sort of like how a cold blast from the hair-dryer smooths your hair."
