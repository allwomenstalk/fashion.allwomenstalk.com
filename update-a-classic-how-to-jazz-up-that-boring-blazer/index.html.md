# Update a Classic: How to Jazz up That Boring Blazer ...

A classic blazer is a wardrobe staple that every woman should own. It's a must for a smart but relaxed look and is perfect for a casual weekend outfit. But are you getting a touch bored with your blazer? This piece is far more versatile than you may think, so try these different ways of styling it …

## 1. Cinch It in with a Belt

![Cinch It in with a Belt](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/kw/hs/szntob6d55447d93358f4877557752_464x696.jpg)

If your blazer is more fitted, try cinching it in with a belt. This can give it quite a different look from wearing it loose or simply buttoned up. Because your blazer won't have any belt loops, you should choose a belt that isn't too long; instead, you'll need one that is only a little longer than your waist measurement.

## 2. Roll up Your Sleeves and Show off the Lining

![Roll up Your Sleeves and Show off the Lining](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/tt/i3/ns04yat455447db22d242675548555_600x788.jpg)

Does your blazer have an interesting lining? Roll up the sleeves and show it off as a contrast to the outside fabric. If the lining is a similar color, make some fake cuffs and pin them to the inside. Fabric in a striped or bold pattern would be great for this. You could have a different look for your blazer every day!

## 3. Add a Trim to the Lapel

![Add a Trim to the Lapel](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/xy/tc/u2xfe5zk55447e55c17f1541482409_600x965.jpg)

For a more permanent embellishment, why not add a trim to the lapels of your blazer? Check out the haberdashery section of your local stores or markets. You'll find lots of fabulous trims there. Sequins, velvet ribbon, or a patterned braid would look great. Or you could go for something really bold, like a feather or fur trim. Be imaginative!

## 4. Dye It

![Dye It](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ui/xr/jyd31w7155447ea72c762182136836_585x1170.jpg)

If your blazer is a lighter color, try dying it (don't bother if it's polyester, as that doesn't take well to dye). Or if it's a darker shade you could bleach it. For a dramatic patterned effect, scrunch the blazer up and leave it in bleach. You'll get a random pattern that will look really cool.

## 5. Pop It over a Dress

![Pop It over a Dress](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/wf/zr/swm3ap0c55447eca229a7434535132_600x900.jpg)

We usually pair our blazer with jeans or pants, but you can give it a different look by throwing it on over a printed dress. This gives it a more casual and fun feel. Or you could wear it with a dress in one bright color. You can even wear your blazer with a pair of tailored shorts for a fresh look.

## 6. Add a Vintage Brooch

![Add a Vintage Brooch](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/hp/tf/z9bisa6d55447ee8cc2f9857556504_600x900.jpg)

Have you ever browsed the accessories in your local vintage or thrift store? There are plenty of fun and stylish brooches that will add interest to a plain blazer. Look for a piece that's glittery or in a fun shape. You want something that will stand out against your blazer and draw the eye to it.

## 7. Get out Your Scissors and Deconstruct It

![Get out Your Scissors and Deconstruct It](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ly/re/uv9fpb5b55447f16236ce509714839_600x900.jpg)

Finally, if you've really had enough of your boring blazer, get out your scissors and deconstruct it! Fray the edges, slash it, or rip the sleeves off altogether. You could add a patch of bright fabric inside to show through a slash. Deconstructed clothes make regular appearances on catwalks, so make your own, much cheaper version.

While blazers are a useful piece to have in your wardrobe, they can get a little dull. But with accessories and a few simple touches you can make your blazer work harder and give you lots of different looks. Which wardrobe staple could you never do without?
