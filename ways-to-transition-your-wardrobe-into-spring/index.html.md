# 7 Ways to Transition Your Wardrobe into Spring ...

Spring is on its way so now is the time to think about different ways to transition your wardrobe into spring. Shed your heavy winter layers and start thinking about ways to lighten up your wardrobe. Transitioning your wardrobe into spring can be as simple as adding a few statement accessories or refreshing your colour palette. Check out the following ways to transition your wardrobe into spring.

## 1. Get Layering

One of the key ways to transition your wardrobe into spring is to get creative with your layering. When you’re not sure what the weather is going to be doing, layers will form a key part of keeping your look weather-appropriate. When transitioning your wardrobe this season, try layering a cool vest over a long sleeved dress for one of the hottest looks right now. Otherwise, you can’t go wrong with plenty of thin layers that you can add or subtract as needed.

## 2. Say It with Accessories

Every season there are certain key pieces that tend to come in fashion. An easy way to transition your wardrobe into spring is to pick up on these trend-based pieces and start adding them to your outfits. Right now, things like colourful statement shoes, holographic accessories, and two-toned bags are your best bets for working the latest spring trends.

## 3. Add Spring Pieces

Transition your wardrobe into spring by adding in some traditional spring pieces. Things like floral dresses and faded denim scream spring dressing. Layer them under coats and with warmer pieces like scarves and stockings to make them more weather appropriate.

## 4. Just Add Colour

Spring wardrobes usually consist of more colour than winter wardrobes. Transition your wardrobe into spring by ditching the dark and dreary hues. Start embracing soft neutrals and bright pops of colours. You can still wear black and white but this spring the trend is for entirely monochrome outfits or bold black and white patterns.

## 5. Show Some Skin

When the weather warms up, ditch the tights and stockings. Show off your legs in a pretty dress or skirt. If the weather is still a bit cool, make sure you have plenty of layers up on top and keep your feet warm. If you still feel the need to wear tights or stockings, go for sheerer styles.

## 6. Try a Trend at a Time

Monochrome, retro, floral, stripes – there are plenty of spring trends to choose from right now. Avoid being a total fashion victim and try out your trends one at a time. It’s a simple yet effective way of easing into spring fashion. It will also help you spread out your outfits so that you don’t go through all of your potential spring looks in a short amount of time.

## 7. Put into Storage

Cut down the ‘noise’ in your wardrobe by putting away clothes that you won’t need for the season ahead. Start putting away the majority of your thick jumpers and heavy coats. Leave one or two pieces out, like the ones you wear a lot, for those extra chilly days. Otherwise, stick to wearing lots of lightweight pieces that you can layer up or down depending on the weather.

Transitioning your wardrobe between seasons can be tricky, but hopefully these tips will make the process a whole lot easier. Transitioning your wardrobe into spring is all about breathing some new life into your wardrobe. What are your best tips for transitioning your wardrobe into spring?
