# 7 Tips on How to Stylishly Wear Tuxedos ...

Want to stay warm and stylish? The tuxedo is the perfect trend for you. Get it right, and it’s timeless, chic and flattering; so it’s no wonder it’s a big hit with celebrities like Kate Moss and Nicole Scherzinger. All you need is a tuxedo that looks great on you, some killer shoes and the right accessories, and you can always feel confident that you’ve got the perfect outfit on standby.

## 1. Keep Prints Classic

![Keep Prints Classic](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2012/12/111_600x771.jpg)

Want a classic but stand-out tuxedo? Don’t shy away from prints, but opt for something classic to keep the look timeless. Try to keep the print colors standard – think monochromes with a touch of navy or dark green – and opt for simple designs such as symmetrical geometric patterns. Emma Roberts always rocks this look! Avoid anything animal-print, and keep the pyjama prints for pyjamas!

## 2. Slicked Hair

![Slicked Hair](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2012/12/25_418x594.jpg)

Rocking the androgynous look is easy, and oh-so-stylish. Get it right by putting your hair into a tight bun at the back, and using plenty of hair gel and gloss spray to get that really slick look. Then add feminine charm with smoky eyes and nude lips. Need inspiration? Check out Karolina Kurkova.

## 3. Opt for Interesting Shirts

![Opt for Interesting Shirts](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2012/12/34_600x920.jpg)

Something else Karolina does well – choosing the right shirt. If your suit is bold and patterned, that doesn’t mean you should stick to a plain white shirt. Instead, find a shirt with cotton and sheer panels, or eye-catching buttons. L’Wren Scott’s famous camis are another great twist, although make sure you are fully covered to keep the smart look – a low neckline will show too much and spoil the look.

## 4. Go White

![Go White](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2012/12/44_350x700.jpg)

A white suit is contemporary, and so easy to accessorize. Kate Hudson owned this look at a screening of Glee, wearing a white Alexander McQueen tuxedo with black lapels and a striped black vest top, along with slick hair and plenty of nude make up. White looks gorgeous with gold and bronze jewelry too.

## 5. Mix and Match

[img.allw.mn](https://img.allw.mn/content/fashion/2012/12/5_400x640.jpeg)

Don’t fancy an all-black or all-white look? Play with your colors instead. Gucci create a selection of colorful tux jackets for this very reason, and there are always some great high-street versions. My favorite look? Emma Stone’s black trousers and bright green jacket. It’s all brought together by her untied shirt and gold chain, making her look absolutely great – and low effort, too.

## 6. Go Vintage

![Go Vintage](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2012/12/62_592x888.jpg)

Loving the retro look? Add vintage charm to your outfit a la Marcia Cross by opting for a classic pussy-bow blouse, and adding some shimmering brooches. Her loose wavy hair completes that classic glamour look perfectly.

## 7. Add Leather

![Add Leather](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2012/12/75_300x580.jpg)

Okay, so leather and tuxedos might not sound like they’d go together – but they really will. Choose a tailored white tuxedo jacket, and add some black leather trousers and open boots. A studded handbag, loose wavy hair and bright lipstick will finish the look, and you’ll look every inch the star – just like leather-loving Cat Deeley, who regularly rocks this look.

So now you know how to wear tuxedos, what are you waiting for? Hit the shops to find a style you love – and make sure it fits perfectly. Tailored designs are often the best choice, especially if you can be measured for adjustments, and don’t forget to check out the colored jackets for a change, too. How do you wear your tux? I’d love to know!
