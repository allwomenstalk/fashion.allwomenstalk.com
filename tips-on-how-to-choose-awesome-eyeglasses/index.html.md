# 8 Tips on How to Choose Awesome Eyeglasses ...

How to Choose Eyeglasses that suit you isn't an inborn trait. It's pretty much trial and error, and the process begins the first time you try on that first pair. As someone who's been wearing specs since she was 12, I can attest to the fact that sometimes, in spite of your best efforts, you end up with glasses that don't look good. The hardest part of learning how to choose eyeglasses is realizing that trends won't always work for you. In order to spare some of you the fashion pangs that come from wearing unsuitable glasses, here are some tips on how to choose eyeglasses that flatter you.

## 1. Face Shape

![Face Shape](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/1_face-shape_440x560.jpg)

When learning how to choose eyeglasses, the first thing you need to consider is the shape of your face. Quite often, the shape of your face can echo the shape of your glasses. For instances, I have an oval, verging on round, facial shape, and those trendy square glasses just don't look good on me. They do, however, look good on learner, more rectangular faces, although there are exceptions, of course. The rule of thumb is that if it looks good on you, it's fine.

## 2. Frames

![Frames](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/2_frames_387x560.jpg)

The frames naturally make all the difference, but let's talk about smaller things before we get on the subject of size and things. Shape is important as well, as mentioned above. Try on different pairs, round, oval, rectangular, square, and so on, to see which looks best on you.

## 3. Size of Frames

![Size of Frames](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/3_size-of-frames_415x560.jpg)

Specifically, the size of your frames play a huge consideration when you're figuring out how to choose eyeglasses. Large frames on a small face throw off the balance of your features, as do small frames on a large place. Figure out what works best for you; odds are it will be something in between.

## 4. Facial Features

![Facial Features](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/4_facial-features_373x560.jpg)

As briefly mentioned, you have to think about your facial features as well. What will best bring out your snub nose or heart shaped chin? You can also use your glasses to bring out your best feature. If you have blue eyes, go with blue, silver, or black frames.

## 5. Body Scale

![Body Scale](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/5_body-scale_443x560.jpg)

You need to think about the rest of your body when learning how to choose eyeglasses, too. The way your body is proportioned might not seem like a big deal, but it is. The right pair of glasses can keep everything beautifully in balance.

## 6. Personal Style

![Personal Style](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/6_personal-style_358x560.jpg)

Of course your personal style comes in to play as well. Sometimes, you just have to try the latest trends or wear something that suits your personality best. If you can carry it off with confidence and if it complements your style, you won't have anything to worry about.

## 7. Thickness of Lenses

![Thickness of Lenses](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/7_thickness-of-lenses_425x560.jpg)

Here's one of those tedious little details people don't often think about, yet it plays a big part in how to choose eyeglasses that suit you. If you have to wear really thick lenses, then you're going to need frames that do heavier duty. This can mean the difference between choosing metal frames over lightweight plastic ones, for instance.

## 8. Check Your Eyelashes

![Check Your Eyelashes](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/fashion/2011/09/8_check-your-eyelashes_460x336.jpg)

What? I know this sounds weird, but if you've ever taken off your glasses only to discover there are little butterfly streaks on the lenses, courtesy of your lashes, you'll understand. Make sure your frames fit well enough that they leave room for your eyelashes. At the very least, that will save you a lot of cleaning time.

There you have it – the most important tricks on how to choose eyeglasses that flatter your features and complement your style. I told you it was easy. The only difficult thing about learning how to choose eyeglasses is remembering to follow all the tips. As a permanent member of the specs-club, I promise that these help. Do you have any other tips on how to choose eyeglasses?
