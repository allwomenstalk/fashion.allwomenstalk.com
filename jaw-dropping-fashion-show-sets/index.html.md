# 9 Jaw\-Dropping Fashion Show Sets You Won't Believe Actually Happened

The clothes are the main focus at any fashion show, but these days it’s becoming more common for fashion show sets to be just as intricately designed as the clothes. While many attend these events to see the **jaw dropping outfits**, the environments they are presented in are often just as captivating. The fashion show is a spectacle in itself, and larger-than-life fashion show sets add to that sense of drama and theatre. Every season, designers seem to up the ante as fashion show sets become more out of this world and theatrical. The following are just a few jaw-dropping fashion show sets worth checking out.

Before we dive into the list, here are a few things that make a fashion show set truly unforgettable:

- Immersive environments that transport the audience to another world.

- Unconventional locations like the Gobi Desert or historical landmarks.

- Interactive elements that models must navigate, like melting icebergs.

## 1. Chanel Fall 2014 Ready-to-Wear

![Chanel Fall 2014 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/c4/i5/jsxhfd2o_600x902.jpg)

Chanel has gained quite a reputation for putting together some jaw-dropping fashion show sets. In the past, we’ve seen windmills, larger-than-life globes, and epic crystal creations decorate the runways. More recently, we saw Karl Lagerfeld unveil a Chanel supermarket that acted as the backdrop to the designer label’s Fall 2014 collection. Models strutted down the aisles, which were packed with Chanel-branded products. You can visit the official [Chanel website](https://www.chanel.com) to see more of their recent collections.

## 2. Louis Vuitton Fall 2012 Ready-to-Wear

![Louis Vuitton Fall 2012 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/i6/mc/0skutbgx_600x901.jpg)

Louis Vuitton also knows what it takes to create a fashion spectacle. For the Fall 2012 ready-to-wear collection, a real train, built just for the occasion, pulled into the Cour Carrée at the Louvre where the show was taking place. Models walked out of the train, wearing the latest [Louis Vuitton collection](https://fashion.allwomenstalk.com/luxury-handbags-every-woman-should-own/), and were accompanied by porters who also carried Louis Vuitton luggage. Talk about making an entrance!

## 3. Christian Dior Fall 2005 Couture

![Christian Dior Fall 2005 Couture](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/yf/pk/lqm76w3f_600x873.jpg)

Floral motifs have long played a role in the Dior brand, but for the Fall 2005 couture collection, the runway took on a darker and more mysterious atmosphere. John Galliano’s vision consisted of a cobweb-covered garden complete with broken statues and overgrown greenery. It was the perfect setting for the almost ghostly and gothic creations that he sent the models down the runway in. This era of [Dior](https://www.dior.com) remains one of the most talked-about in fashion history.

## 4. Pierre Cardin Spring 2008 Ready-to-Wear

![Pierre Cardin Spring 2008 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/cq/wy/lwc4aam0_400x466.jpg)

Sometimes nature provides the best backdrops. Pierre Cardin proved this to be true by using the Gobi Desert in China as a backdrop to showcase their spring/summer 2008 collection. The collection’s clean, minimal designs stood out against the epic backdrop of desert sand dunes, much like the contrast seen in some [iconic fashion moments](https://fashion.allwomenstalk.com/iconic-fashion-moments/) over the years.

## 5. Fendi Spring 2008 Ready-to-Wear

![Fendi Spring 2008 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/q8/be/kd13m7m1_450x793.jpg)

Fendi made history when it hosted a runway show on the Great Wall of China. 88 models walked down a stretch of the Great Wall, showcasing the label’s spring/summer 2008 collection. Guests were seated down the sides of the runway, lining the edges of the Great Wall. This remains one of the most ambitious [Fendi](https://www.fendi.com) events ever staged.

## 6. Marc Jacobs Fall 2012 Ready-to-Wear

![Marc Jacobs Fall 2012 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/do/ey/f9h72ql3_500x703.jpg)

For his Fall 2012 show, Marc Jacobs enlisted his friend Rachel Feinstein to help come up with the set design. What transpired was a surreal backdrop that looked like crumbling ruins. What made the set even more interesting was that it was made mainly out of paper and wood. This show is often cited alongside other [influential fashion designers](https://fashion.allwomenstalk.com/most-influential-fashion-designers/) for its creativity.

## 7. McQ Alexander McQueen Fall 2012 Ready-to-Wear

![McQ Alexander McQueen Fall 2012 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/rs/fl/fep1ed8k_600x600.jpg)

The set at the McQ Alexander McQueen Fall 2012 show resembled a hazy, wooded forest. A carpet of dried leaves decorated the runways which added to the autumnal atmosphere. This is quite something when you remember that collections are shown a season in advance and crunchy, dried leaves would have been quite rare at the time. Much like the [Dior cobweb garden](#3), this set relied heavily on atmospheric textures.

## 8. Chanel Fall 2010 Ready-to-Wear

![Chanel Fall 2010 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/ct/cd/bq94ibi9_600x900.jpg)

One of the most ambitious feats that Chanel has achieved was importing a 265-tonne iceberg onto the runway of their fall 2010 ready-to-wear show. Yes, a real iceberg. The models donned plenty of clothing appropriate for Arctic conditions and as the show went on the iceberg melted, creating puddles that the models had to strut through. This was even more daring than the [Chanel supermarket](#1) we mentioned earlier.

## 9. Givenchy Spring 2014 Ready-to-Wear

![Givenchy Spring 2014 Ready-to-Wear](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/0s/em/dmmvvu2k_600x600.jpg)

Guests probably weren’t expecting to see a car wreck when they arrived at Givenchy’s Spring 2014 runway show. The multi-vehicle pile-up, complete with smoke, sat at the center of a circular runway. The installation added a dangerous element to the show and complemented the darker feel of the collection. You can explore more modern looks on the [Givenchy official site](https://www.givenchy.com).

Sometimes we end up remembering the fashion show sets more than the clothes themselves. These are just a few larger-than-life sets that designers have used as a backdrop to their collections. Do you have any favorite fashion show sets?
