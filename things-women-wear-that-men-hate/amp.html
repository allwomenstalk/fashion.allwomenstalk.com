20 Things Women Wear That Men Hate ...

By Carly

On the whole, it is my belief that women dress for themselves more than they dress to impress peers and love interests, but there is no denying that we all have our moments when our primary goal is to catch someone’s attention in an exciting and romantic way! It is for these life moments that you need to know that there are definitely a few things that guys don’t like. Here are 20 things women wear that men hate!

1 Bold Floral Prints

They might look sophisticated and summery to us, but the truth is that most men associate big floral prints with old ladies and women in their family like their mother and their grandmothers!

2 Jumpsuits

Guys don’t really get jumpsuits; they see them as kind of stylised work clothes or boiler suits, even if they do help to show off your natural curves!

***

However, women continue to don these one-piece wonders for their ease and fashion-forward vibes. They come in various fabrics and cuts, from chic silk to casual cotton and with tapered legs or wide-leg options, catering to different tastes and body types. The confusion for men might stem from the garment's versatility – is it casual wear, is it formal, is it for a night out? In the end, though, many women aren't dressing for the male gaze but for their own comfort and style. Jumpsuits are here to stay, and ladies rocking them with confidence only makes them more attractive.

3 Peplums

This is a trend that very much pays homage to the past, and guys don’t tend to enjoy traditional fashion references; they instead just see them as old fashioned.

4 Floppy Hats

Floppy hats are another fashion trend that evoke a more old fashioned image in a guy’s mind. They don’t tend to understand nods to the 20s and 30s.

***

These wide-brimmed accessories may scream summer chic to us, with their ability to conjure images of leisurely beach days or elegant garden parties. However, to many men, they come across as impractical and excessive. They might see it as a barrier to intimacy, feeling that the prominent hat creates a sense of distance rather than an inviting look. While we adore the mystery and the je ne sais quoi a floppy hat adds, it seems that some guys prefer a more straightforward and accessible style that doesn't require them to navigate around our fashionable sun-shielding choices.

5 Open Side Shirts

Let’s be honest, open-sided shirts aren’t a great look on men or women; it’s not the sexiest sight in the world to see someone’s bare middle and nothing else!

6 Platforms

Platform shoes might give you a feeling of girl power, but in a guy’s mind, heels and stilettos are much sexier!

7 Wedge Sneakers

Again, these might have worked for the Spice Girls, but men really don’t understand that kind of fashion and wedge sneakers just look weird to them!

8 Ultra Heels

But in the same breath, you don’t want to overdo it with the height of your heels. Guy’s don’t always love it when their date is taller than them!

9 Pointy Toed Shoes

Shoes that boast super pointy toes tend to remind guys of strict, nasty teachers that they might have had in their youth!

***

These sharp-tipped silhouettes can give off an intimidating vibe rather than a welcoming one, making them less than appealing to many men. In the fashion realm, though, they've had their moments of glory, but from a male perspective, the overly exaggerated point can seem less chic and more witchy. Opt for rounded or almond-shaped toes if you're aiming for a look that's soft and inviting, one that won't leave guys feeling like they're about to be disciplined for unfinished homework! Remember, it's all about finding that balance between style and approachability.

10 Bangles

Wearing more than one bangle on your wrist can annoy a guy who isn’t accustomed to the jangling sound that always occurs.

11 High Waisted Clothes

High waisted bottoms can be very high fashion, but they can also mess with perception of your natural curves which can be confusing to a guy who doesn’t care about couture!

***

While high waisted trousers and skirts elongate the legs and accentuate the waistline, many men find this style perplexing due to the alteration of body proportions. The unconventional silhouette often conceals the natural waist-hip transition that more traditional rises showcase. For men unconcerned with the latest fashion trends, the shift away from a natural-looking figure, even in the name of style, can disrupt their sense of aesthetics. But fashion-forward women might argue that dressing isn't about catering to the male gaze – it's about personal style and confidence.

12 Mullet Dresses

In a guy’s mind, why would you show off the front of your legs if you didn’t want to show off the back of them too?

13 Menswear

Not all guys like the tomboy look. It can sometimes put them off if you turn for a date wearing pretty much the same as they are!

14 Neon Makeup

We love a bit of 80s homage, but guys tend to find neon makeup looks a little bit over the top.

15 Maxi Dresses

A guy who is interested in you wants to be able to see your curves, so a maxi dress is definitely his worst enemy!

16 Granny Chic

Granny chic is basically lots of loose knits and wool, but I think you might be able to understand that anything named after a granny might not be a favourite of a guy who is trying to get into your pants!

17 Blazers

Blazers are awesome for the office, but sometimes wearing one out on a date can make the guy feel like you are still in a professional mood rather than fun mood.

18 Shoulder Pads

Again, shoulder pads might be back in fashion, but for most guys they still evoke memories of their mothers and grannies!

19 Hipster Excess

Guys enjoy a little bit of quirkiness, but they don’t often enjoy it if you go full blown hipster on them!

***

While a touch of individual style can certainly catch a guy’s eye, an overload of what's considered trendy in the hipster realm can quickly become a turn-off. It’s all about striking that delicate balance. Sure, your vintage-inspired glasses or that thrifted coat can be cute and charming in moderation, but piling on layer upon layer of eclectic accessories and apparel might just overshadow your personal charm. So, rock that hipster vibe if it feels right, just remember that less is often more when it’s about making a genuine connection.

20 Glamazon

Try to tone it down a little bit when it comes to the full glamour look. You don’t want your man to think that you look more like a drag queen than yourself!
