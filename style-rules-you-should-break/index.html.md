# Five Style Rules You Need to Break to Look Your Best ...

Now, doesn't that sound scandalous right off the bat? As someone who's been navigating the treacherous waters of fashion for years, let me tell you, those sacred rules that fashion gurus beat into our brains might just be holding you back. I mean, it’s 2024, and the last thing you want is to look like you're stuck in a time capsule.

**Fashion rules** have been famously stringent, yet surprisingly outdated. Remember when mixing prints was considered a fashion faux-pas? Been there, done that, and guess what? Mixing prints is now _the_ thing! And let’s face it, we’ve all fallen victim to those harsh words of style advice from TV shows or magazines that dictate our every wardrobe decision. In this article, we’re going to debunk some of those myths and go over why breaking these rules will make you feel like a _style guru_.

Now, before you grab your pitchforks, let me kindle some curiosity. Ever tried pairing those white jeans with a summer outfit and someone said it's a faux-pas unless it's after Memorial Day? News flash: those _linen white pants_ look just as chic on a cool October day. Speaking of which, did you know that some iconic fashionistas regularly break the traditional _seasonal rules_? And can we talk about how I once wore white sneakers to a winter event and didn't turn into a pumpkin at midnight?

Ah, winter events, they remind me-_winter layering_ is another burning topic we’ll tackle. Trust me when I say that merging practicality with fashion can be your best move. For instance, [mixing high and low fashion](#3) might just become your newest lifesaver. We all love a splurge, but a savvy shopper knows that pairing a designer coat with budget-friendly boots doesn’t just save money; it looks fabulous and keeps you warm too!

Of course, let’s not forget color coordination-another biggie that many are cautious about. Guess what? Wearing _bright colors_ on a gloomy day can lift not just your mood but also your entire look. So, why stick with the drab greys and blacks when you can splash on some lively hues?

To bring this introduction to a jubilant end, here’s a spoiler: embracing your unique fashion sense is much more empowering than fitting into someone else's mold. If you’ve been rigidly following outdated fashion guidelines, consider this your freeing moment. Buckle up, because we're diving into [breaking the rules with confidence](#4) and tapping into your authentic style!

So stay tuned, my fashion-forward reader, because you’re about to discover why some rules are just meant to be broken.

## 1. BUYING JUST BECAUSE IT is on SALE

More often than not, malls and retail shops lure us into their thresholds by proclaiming a percentage off their merchandise. In an effort to get us to buy their stuff, they promise us saving more than what we would have had the particular item not been on sale. But the reality is, even if the tag proclaims you have just saved fifty percent, you just really spent money-you have not saved at all. While this is all well and good if you actually do love the item, it is another thing entirely if you just bought it on account that it is on sale. What happens to that slinky dress that you do not really want but was on sale then? Chances are, you just wasted your money on a fashion piece you do not really see yourself wearing.

## 2. DROWNING in OVERSIZED ITEMS

While oversized sartorial items are great and are trendy nowadays, this does not mean you have to entirely engulf yourself in these items. At least create a balance between well-fitting clothes and blousy and loose ones. Additionally, you need to be a little discriminating as well. Remember, there is a significant difference between wearing something oversized and trying to fit into clothes four times bigger than your regular size.

## 3. BURYING YOURSELF in ACCESSORIES

Accessories are great, and they work to your advantage by creating interest in your ensemble-particularly if you are donning rather plain clothes. But be careful not to overdo it and pile too many of them on. While bling is great and makes your outfit look unique, there is no need to overwhelm yourself with it. You want your outfit to look tasteful, not trashy. Furthermore, burying yourself in too many accessories would make you seem like an over adorned Christmas tree and no one looks good like that.

## 4. OVERDOING REPETITIVE PRINTS

Today, coordinates are all the rage, and they look nothing short of fabulous. However, this does not mean that you have to wear it from head-to-toe-even extending to your shoes, your accessories and your bag. You want to look fashionable and stylish, not like you are a walking ad for a certain product.

## 5. TRYING TOO HARD

There is absolutely no point in looking stylish if you are simply doing it just for the sake of being called such. If anything, it makes you seem like you are trying too hard. Do not force an ensemble just because you think you will look stylish. No matter how fashion experts may exalt a particular outfit in a magazine, if it does not work on the streets, chances are it is not going to work for you. Remember, take what you read in fashion magazines with a grain of salt-you live in reality after all, and not on the fashion runway.
